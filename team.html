KCF TEAM

Kevin Mwachiro

Creative Director

A Kenyan writer, podcaster, queer activist, and journalist whose media and communications career spans more than two decades. Kevin brings a unique perspective to the festival, blending storytelling with activism and cultural preservation. As Creative Director, he shapes the artistic vision of KCF and ensures the festival remains a platform for diverse, meaningful narratives.

Zippy Kimundu

Co-Founder

An award-winning Kenyan filmmaker who has worked in the global film industry for nearly two decades. Zippy is the founder of Afrofilms International, a production and distribution company dedicated to amplifying African stories on the world stage. Her expertise in curation, production, and international film networks is central to KCF's programming.

Mumo Liku

Co-Founder

A multi-disciplinary trained director, producer and curator. Mumo co-founded the Baruu Collective and brings a wealth of experience in creative direction, event curation, and community engagement. His work bridges the gap between contemporary art and grassroots community participation.

Saitabao Kaiyare

Co-Founder

A filmmaker from Kilifi, Kenya with a decade of experience in the film industry. Saitabao co-founded the Baruu Collective and is deeply connected to the coastal community. His local knowledge and filmmaking expertise ensure KCF stays true to its roots while reaching international audiences.

Wakarima Wangui

Festival Manager

Wakarima Wangui is the Festival Manager of Kilifi Creek Festival, bringing strong organizational and logistical expertise to the team. With a background in event management and a deep passion for arts and culture, she ensures the smooth execution of all festival activities — from venue coordination and scheduling to partnerships and on-the-ground operations.
